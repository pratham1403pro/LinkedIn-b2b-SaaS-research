# Research Methodology

## How I Selected the 10 Experts

The goal was to find practitioners who actually use LinkedIn content to grow B2B SaaS businesses — not just people who write about it.

### Step 1 — Define what "high signal" means
I wasn't looking for the most famous names. I was looking for people who:
- Built something real using LinkedIn content (a company, a product, measurable pipeline)
- Have original frameworks or research, not just rephrased advice
- Are specific to B2B SaaS, not general marketing

### Step 2 — Cross-reference generic lists
I collected 5 different "top LinkedIn experts" lists from Google and flagged anyone who appeared on multiple lists. If a name showed up on 2+ generic lists, they were excluded — the goal was to go beyond obvious picks.

### Step 3 — Verify each person individually
For every expert I shortlisted, I checked:
- Do they have real results? (revenue numbers, ARR, client outcomes)
- Are they still actively posting in 2025/2026?
- Do they have YouTube content available for transcript collection?

## How I Collected Content

**LinkedIn posts** — collected manually. LinkedIn blocks automated scraping, so each expert's posts were found and saved by hand. 3 posts per expert were selected based on relevance to LinkedIn content strategy for B2B SaaS.

**YouTube transcripts** — collected via YouTube Data API v3 to fetch video links, then transcripts were extracted using the Supadata API, which returns clean text transcripts directly from YouTube video URLs. 2 videos per expert were collected.
