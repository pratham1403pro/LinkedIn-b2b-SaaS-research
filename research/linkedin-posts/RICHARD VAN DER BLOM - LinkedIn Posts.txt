My gift to you, 6 ingredients of high performing LinkedIn Post that boosted my conversion 3.5x over the last 3 months.

During the research of millions of LinkedIn Post we found that the best performing ones used more or less the same structure (not AI generated). Implementing these elements will engage your human audience in a better way.

Based on 7 years of ongoing research, analyzing over 6M posts in total and writing over 1,000 pages of LinkedIn Insights and Tactics. That's me.

1. Hook
↳ Probably the most important part of the post. Make people click "more"
↳ Use a personal experience as it outperforms any other type of hook
↳ Not possible? Go for a negative hook over a positive one
↳ example: see the first 2 lines of this post

2. Promise
↳ Don't think when people click "more" you have earned their time
↳ They just gave you the benefit of the doubt and 3 seconds more
↳ Tell me in 2 lines why should I continue reading, what value do I get
↳ example: see the 2nd paragraph of this post

3. Positioning
↳ New algorithm, 50% of your viewers come from outside your network now
↳ This might be the first post they see from you
↳ Position yourself, why are you the expert to share this?
↳ example: check the 3rd paragraph of this post

4. Core Message
↳ This is the actual value you wanted to share, the part people actually should read
↳ Use formatting, avoid long sentences or paragraphs
↳ Don't bore, but inspire

5. Conclusion
↳ Want conversion? Never, ever, ever let people draw the conclusion themselves
↳ What should people get from this, make this message clear
↳ This part also helps to warm up your audience for further action

6. Call to Engage (CTE)
↳ Not be be confused with a commercial Call to Action (CTA)
↳ Closed Questions lead to 2,7x more engagement
↳ Open Questions lead to 3,4x more conversations in DM's
↳ example: "Which of the these trends will have the biggest impact"

And I save the best Tip for last.

Don't use this to start writing, because it will look forced.
Instead, draft your post and then check if you can make some natural changes to fit in all ingredients.
