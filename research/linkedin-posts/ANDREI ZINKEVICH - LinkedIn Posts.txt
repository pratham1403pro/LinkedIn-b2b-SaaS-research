"Nobody from my strategic accounts literally knows us". At the same time marketing team is:

- Under-resourced. 2 people are supporting 3 verticals and 3 different sales teams in one region with a partial support from a global marketing team.
- Stretched into multiple activities: sales enablement content, partnerships, signals tracking, events, running AI initiatives.
- Challenged to run campaigns. Because campaigns is the only way to get a visible marketing interaction in CRM to prove marketing-influenced pipeline later.

While sales is running outbound and pushing marketing to generate more leads.

There are 3 problems:

- Trying to create awareness at scale without a methodology.
- Thinking that only marketing is responsible for creating awareness
- Ignoring how buyers actually become aware of different vendors

Fundamental fix is to stop pushing marketing to run into multiple directions and develop a methodology to create brand awareness among strategic accounts. This includes:

- How we select and prioritize accounts
- Research on how the buying groups become aware of different buyers
- Mapping out a buyer ecosystem: how and where you can influence target buyers directly and indirectly
- Aligning global always-on programs with the use cases of strategic accounts to distribute the same messaging across all channels
- Develop time-based and signal-based playbooks that build one on another to have a consistent flow of touchpoints with the target accounts
- Turning the playbooks into weekly todos for marketing and sales

Before blaming marketing for lack of awareness among strategic accounts:

- remove the constraints
- build the methodology
- ensure that creating awareness is actually a strategic priority, not one more task on already full plate of the marketing team

---
That being said, creating awareness in 2026 is WAY HARDER than it was before:

- Higher CPC
- Drop in organic search and organic reach of content on socials.
- AI-based vendor search and evaluation without talking to sales
- AI slop
- Increased volume of outbound emails

Here is one tiny naughty truth about B2B demand generation programs that work....

They are aligned with the 3 levels of demand.

1. DEMAND FOR CONTENT.

Buying intent: -/low.

Example:
I'm a B2B CMO selling high ACV product across a long sales cycle. I've my H2 plan and just interested in learning best practices and stay updated with industry news.

Demand gen goals:
Motivate buyers to:
- engage with your content
- start following you/your company
- signing up for your events
- signing up for the newsletter

Average cycle: 6-8 months.

2. DEMAND FOR SOLUTION.

This is a stage when buyers prioritize challenges and strategic projects, and evaluate different solutions.

Buying intent: low/medium.

Example:
We're missing revenue targets by 20%. Sales complains about lack of awareness among strategic accounts. We're aligned with them in theory, but haven't done anything in practice. What should we do?

Demand gen goals:
- Drive traffic to your use case page
- Nurture with "solution-based" content the entire buying committee

Average cycle: 3-6 months.

3. DEMAND FOR VENDOR.

This is a stage where the buying decision happens. Buyers do vendor research and talk to vetted vendors. The first vendors they select are the vendors that attracted their attention earlier.

Buying intent: high.

Example:
We want to create a unified account-based methodology that will help to align marketing and sales around strategic accounts. Which vendor will help us to create and implement the methodology with our resources and budget?

Demand gen goals:
- Generate sales opportunities

Average cycle: 1–3 months.

----
For many years, our industry believed that buyers move through linear stages: "problem aware - solution aware - vendor aware".

But the truth is I simply don't know people who are not problem aware. It's either they haven't prioritized it or there are organizational constraints that won't allow fixing it.

Another truth: the buyers often become vendor aware way before they become solution aware. So, if we focus only on trying to generate demand for us as a vendor, we are already losing.

Demand for vendor is created way before the problem or strategic project becomes a top priority.
